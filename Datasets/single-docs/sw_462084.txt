Lyndam Gregory

Lyndam Gregory (1953 or 1954 â€“ August 2014) was a British actor. He had 35 credits to his name between 1979 and 2008. He was best known for his roles in the television series "The Archers", "Coronation Street", "EastEnders" and "Doctors".

Gregory was born in Darjeeling, West Bengal, India. His father, Neville, was a tea planter. His mother, Marina, was a Burmese school teacher. He moved to London in the early 1960s. In 1984, he married Christine. Their daughter, Dominique, was born in 1997.

Gregory died from lung cancer in Milton Keynes, Buckinghamshire, aged 59. His death was announced on 21 August 2014.
