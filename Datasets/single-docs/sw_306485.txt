Peter Leko

Peter Leko (; born 8 September 1979 in Subotica, Yugoslavia) is the leading Hungarian chess grandmaster. 

He became a grandmaster in 1994 at the age of 14 years (a world record at the time). He was the challenger in the Classical World Championship 2004 against Vladimir Kramnik. He tied with Kramnik 7-7, but this meant Kramnik retained his title. Leko's best world ranking was fourth, first achieved in April 2003.
