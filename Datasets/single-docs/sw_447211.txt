Catharine Macaulay

Catharine Macaulay(2 April 1731 â€“ 22 June 1791) was an English historian. Between 1763 and 1783 Macaulay wrote her biggest work, "The History of England from the Accession of James I to that of the Brunswick Line". Her 1790 work "Letters on Education" deeply influenced Mary Wollstonecraft.

Macaulay was born Catharine Sawbridge at Olantigh in Kent. She had two brothers. She was educated by a governess.

Macaulay married her first husband George Macaulay on 18 June 1760. In 1766 he died. In 1774 she moved to Bath. In 1788 Macaulay married William Graham. Macaulay died in Binfield in 1791.
