SEPECAT Jaguar

The SEPECAT Jaguar is a twin-turbojet military aircraft. It was designed and built as an attack aircraft, also used as a training aircraft. It was built by SEPECAT, an Anglo-French company. It first flew in 1968. "Armée de l'Air" and Royal Air Force were the main users. It was used in wars in Africa by French, and in Gulf War and Afghanistan War by British and French.
It's also built under licence in India as Jaguar International.

Apart "Armée de l'Air" and Royal Air Force the Jaguar was purchased by Ecuador, India, Nigeria and Oman.
