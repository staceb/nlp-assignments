Foz do Iguaçu

Foz do Iguaçu (fɔs do igwa'su) is the 4th largest city of Paraná state, in the south region of Brazil. 309,000 people live there. It is about west of Curitiba, Parana's capital city, on the border with Argentina and Paraguay. The people who live there are known as "iguaçuenses".

The city is known for the Iguaçu Falls and the Itaipu Dam, the largest dam of the world was built.
